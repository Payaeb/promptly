# Privacy Policy for Promptly

**Last updated: March 25, 2026**

## Overview

Promptly is a Firefox browser extension that helps you send web page content to Claude.ai with pre-configured prompts. Your privacy is important and this policy explains exactly what data Promptly handles.

## Data Collection

Promptly does NOT collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external servers.

## What Promptly Stores

All data is stored locally in your browser using Firefox's built-in storage API:

- **Button configurations**: The names, prompts, and settings for your custom buttons
- **Backlog items**: URLs or text selections you add to a button's queue
- **Project references**: Claude.ai project IDs you configure in button settings

This data never leaves your browser. It is not sent to any server, analytics service, or third party.

## What Promptly Accesses

When you click a button, Promptly:

1. Reads the current tab's URL or your selected text (only when you click a button)
2. Opens a new tab on claude.ai with your configured prompt

When you use the project picker in settings, Promptly:

1. Connects to claude.ai using your existing logged-in session to fetch your project list
2. This data is only used to populate the project dropdown and is not stored beyond your button configuration

## Third-Party Services

Promptly only interacts with claude.ai (operated by Anthropic). It does not communicate with any other external service.

## Data Sharing

Promptly does not share any data with anyone. The export feature creates a local JSON file on your computer that you control.

## Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be included with the extension update.

## Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, please open an issue on the extension's support page.
